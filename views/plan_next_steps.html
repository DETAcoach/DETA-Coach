The Coach will help you think about how the answer to your research question will inform the decisions you make regarding your technology. To complete this tool you will need to consider:

  • What would success look like?
  • How much risk are you willing to take when making a decision or a recommendation?

Sections A through D will help you think about these questions. Remember, you can always come back to this step and change your answers. In other words, nothing you enter here is permanent, so just fill this out as best you can.

A. What is the cost of <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>?

It about $ per to use <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>.

If the sentence above does not fit, you can describe the cost of your technology in a couple of sentences instead.

Things to Consider

The cost of a technology could simply be the cost per user. It could also be the savings realized by replacing one technology with another less expensive technology. Cost can also be measured in something other than dollars, such as teacher preparation time saved.

Example

  • It costs about $20 per student to use the educational technology.
  • It saves about $1,000 per classroom to use the educational technology.
  • We're not sure. We believe using this tool will save teachers 20 minutes per day on prep time.

Things to Consider

Things to Consider

We rarely have enough evidence to be completely certain that a technology will work if implemented well. Thinking forward to your results, what likelihood (or probability) would you need to be comfortable concluding that the educational technology is moving the needle? You might want a higher level of certainty, and therefore a higher probability, if your results will affect a higher stakes decision, such as implementing a district-wide curriculum. But you might be comfortable with a lower probability for a lower stakes decision.
