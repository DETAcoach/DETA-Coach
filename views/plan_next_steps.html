Think About How You Will Use The Results

The Coach will help you think about how the answer to your research question will inform the decisions you make regarding your technology. What would success look like? How much risk are you willing to take when making a decision or a recommendation? For example, if implementing a technology across your district is very costly, you may want the technology to have a large impact to justify the expense. Moreover, you will want to be very certain that any effect observed can be attributed to using the technology. On the other hand, if the technology is free or will save money, you may just want to be confident that changing to this technology won’t hurt outcomes and you may be willing to accept more uncertainty about the effects.

Sections A through D will help you think about these questions. Remember, you can always come back to this step and change your answers. In other words, nothing you enter here is permanent, so just fill this out as best you can.

A. What is the cost of <%= Basics_Tech_Name %>

It approximately $ per to use <%= Basics_Tech_Name %>?

If the sentence above does not fit your technology, you can describe the cost in a couple of sentences instead.

Things to Consider

The cost of a technology could simply be the cost per user. It could also be the savings realized by replacing one technology with another less expensive technology. Cost may also be measured in something other than dollars, such as teacher prep time saved.

Example

  • It costs approximately $20 per student to use the educational technology
  • It saves approximately $1,000 per classroom to use the educational technology
  • We're not sure. We believe using this tool will save teachers 20 minutes per day on prep time

You may want a higher level of certainty, and therefore a higher probability, if your results will impact a higher stakes decision, such as implementing a district-wide curriculum. But you may be comfortable with a lower probability for a lower stakes decision.
