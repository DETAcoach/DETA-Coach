The Coach will help you think about how the answer to your research question will inform the decisions you make regarding your technology. To complete this tool you will need to consider:

  • What would success look like?
  • How much risk are you willing to take when making a decision or a recommendation?

Sections A through D will help you think about these questions. Remember, you can always come back to this step and change your answers. In other words, nothing you enter here is permanent, so just fill this out as best you can.

A. What is the cost of <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>?

It about $ per to use <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>.

If the sentence above does not fit, you can describe the cost of your technology in a couple of sentences instead.

Things to Consider

The cost of a technology could simply be the cost per user. It could also be the savings realized by replacing one technology with another less expensive technology. Cost can also be measured in something other than dollars, such as teacher preparation time saved.

Example

  • It costs about $20 per student to use the educational technology.
  • It saves about $1,000 per classroom to use the educational technology.
  • We're not sure. We believe using this tool will save teachers 20 minutes per day on prep time.

Think about your goals for <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>. How large of a change would you need to see in order to conclude that the <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %> is having the intended effect and is preferable to the alternative? This is called the minimum meaningful effect or MME.

For this evaluation, is a(n) <%= defs.Outcome_Direction %> of any amount in <%= defs.Basics_Outcome %> meaningful?

I would consider any <%= defs.Outcome_Direction %> at least units on <%= defs.Outcome_Measure %> a success.

The Coach will use your answer to this question to divide the range of possible effects into three regions—one where the two groups are equivalent, one where treatment group performs better than the comparison group by an amount at least as large as the MME, and one where the treatment group performs worse than the comparison group by an amount at least as large as the MME. Things to Consider

  • The MME will be different for different situations. For example, if you’re looking at a free app, you may be happy with any positive change—in this case the MME is 0. But if you’re considering a substantial and costly change to a curriculum, you may want to see a larger difference between the treatment and comparison groups. In that case, you might choose 5 points on your assessment as the MME.
  • Remember that the Coach will compare average outcomes for the intervention group to average outcomes for the comparison group. This difference is what will be compared to the MME.
  • To choose the MME, consider the range of values for that outcome. If it’s unusual to see differences of 10 points between groups of students, then 10 might be too high.
  • If you hope to see an increase in a reading test score, would a 1 point increase, on average, be a meaningful increase? What about 5 points?
  • Make sure the number you select here matches the magnitude of the outcomes in your data. For example, if your units are percent, you could use values between 0 and 1 or between 0 and 100. If you use values between 0 and 1 in your dataset you should put a value between 0 and 1 here.

C. How much certainty do you need?

The Coach will use your data to calculate how likely it is that <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %> is meeting your goals. It is very unlikely that the Coach will be able to tell you this with 100% certainty, so you have to decide how much uncertainty you are willing accept when making a decision based on this analysis.

You make these types of decisions all the time, for example, when you decide whether or not to take an umbrella after looking at the day’s weather forecast. Some people will take an umbrella if the probability of rain is 10%, others might only take an umbrella if a 60% chance is forcasted. That is, different people have different thresholds for how much risk they are willing to take. And you may have a different threshold for different kinds of decision.

What certainty threshold do you want to use for this case? Fill in this number in the statement below:

Things to Consider

  • The default setting is 75%, but you should consider whether this is appropriate for your decision.
  • You might want a higher level of certainty, and therefore a higher probability, if your results will affect a higher stakes decision, such as implementing a district-wide curriculum. But you might be comfortable with a lower probability for a lower stakes decision.
  • If you choose a probability that is too high, for example 100 the Coach will likely conclude that there is not enough evidence.
  • The Coach will calculate this probability based on the data you provide. You should only believe these findings to the extent you believe you have a good comparison group in the data.
