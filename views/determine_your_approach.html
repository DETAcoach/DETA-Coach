Things to Consider

From a study design point of view, the best way to determine who gets the technology (treatment group) and who does not (comparison group) is through a random chance process (like flipping a coin), but it's not the only way.

You do not need to randomly select users at the individual level. You can randomly select classrooms or schools. however, your ability to learn whether the technology is effective will be stronger if you can choose at the individual level.

Do not create your groups based on who volunteers. Those who volunteer are inherently different than those who do not. For example, a technology may seem to move the needle but it could be that only highly tech savvy teachers can take advantage of it and it would be harmful for your other teachers. If you do have volunteers, split them between your treatment and comparison groups.

Conclusion

Based on your answers, the Coach will guide you through a randomized pilot. The choice of who will pilot the technology will be determined by chance, like a coin flip. This design is the gold standard to determine if an education technology is moving the needle because it allows us to create two groups that are similar on both observed and unobserved characteristics. This means that you can be confident that any differences you see in outcomes are due to the technology and not other factors.

Conclusion

Since you use a cutoff to determine who will use the technology, a Regression Discontinuity design (RD) would be a good approach. This design compares outcomes for individuals just above and just below the cutoff to determine whether the technology is having an effect. This works because we assume that those around the cutoff are very similar and that any differences are due to using the technology. However, your conclusions may not apply to those farther away from the cutoff.

The Coach does not yet include tools for RD, but if you're interested in pursuing an evaluation please contact us at EdTechRCE@mathematica-mpr.com and you may be able to use beta versions of tools we are creating to handle this type of design.
