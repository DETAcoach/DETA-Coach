When you share the results from your study, the information you provide on this webpage will help your colleagues understand how the educational technology is intended to be used. We’ll prompt you later in the process to reflect on how the technology was actually implemented and whether you experienced any implementation challenges. This context will help you make sense of the results and it is also important for understanding how the evaluation applies to other environments.

Things to Consider

To answer these questions, you may want to draw from the instructions or training provided by the product developer.

You can always modify this later. If you don’t know the answer to a question, you may want to write something like “ASK STEVE ABOUT THIS” as a place holder.

See the example to see how these responses will appear in your findings brief. Be sure not to include any Personally Identifiable Information, like student or teacher names.

Example

  • TrueStar Reading is software designed to improve reading achievement among under-performing students.
  • TrueStar Reading offers personalized lessons and reading content based on reading level.
  • Students were meant to use TrueStar Reading individually during class time for 90 minutes per week.
  • The evaluation period lasted fourteen weeks.

Things to Consider

Some technologies work well in some settings and not so well in others. Include features of the setting that you think might influence how well you would expect the technology to work. These might have to do with the available technology resources (e.g. computer labs, laptops for students, etc.), student population, or presence of other technology initiatives at schools.

Example

XYZ School District is located in a rural setting where each school in the district has 400 students and class sizes of 20, on average. Each school has 1 computer lab and moderate internet connectivity.

Things to Consider

System data (also sometimes called log data) can provide information about the extent to which the technology was actually used, which may differ from intended use. You can refer to Guide: Summarize Context for more information on how to access system data.

Example

  • According to the vendor, students who complete all 10 modules are most likely to benefit from the education technology. 90% of students attempting the first three modules. Usage dropped dramatically for modules 4-10, with only 5% of students attempted module 10.
  • This technology is designed for teachers to assign online homework exercises that students complete at home, but many students do not have internet access at home. The technology was therefore used primarily as a source of in-class activities.
