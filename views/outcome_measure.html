Define Your Outcome and Measure

An outcome is knowledge, skills, attitudes, or other desired benefits that are attained as a result of an activity (for example, engaging with an educational technology).

There may be numerous types of outcomes that you hope to affect with <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %>. However, for each Coach evaluation you need to select a single outcome as your focus.

This tool will help you think through the outcome you intend to affect and determine how you will measure that outcome.

Things to Consider

  • What do you want to change?
  • Is <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %> aimed at students, teachers, or other users?
  • What specific aspect of their skills, knowledge, behavior, attitudes, etc. is <%= defs.Basics_Tech_Name %> targeting?
  • Over what time frame do you expect to see changes?

We recommend discussing the appropriate outcome with other stakeholders in the district before getting started. The Case Study-Wake County Public School System: Are We Measuring the Right Outcome? illustrates the perils of leaving this discussion until the end of the evaluation.

Our guide to choosing an outcome and measure can help you walk through this decision.

The next step is figuring out how you can measure your outcome. Sometimes this is straightforward, such as using an achievement test to measure student knowledge on a topic, other times it can be more challenging .

Your measure, be it an assessment, survey, or administrative records, must provide you with a quantitative result that reflects the underlying attribute you are trying to affect.

No problem!

How you measure your outcome can be as important as the outcome itself. The following resources can help you choose the best measure to use in your evaluation:

  • See our guide to choosing an outcome and measure for an in-depth look at what matters when selecting a measure.
  • The Find an Instrument to Measure Your Outcome tool also provides guidelines for selecting measures and includes a list of possible instruments that measure common non-academic outcomes.

Things to Consider

  • Does your school or district already measure the outcome?
  • If the outcome can’t be measured directly, are there proxies you could look at?
  • When will you need the data to be available?
  • What type of assessment or survey would work in your context?
  • How much does the instrument cost to use (in terms of both time and money)?
  • Is any expertise required to administer the instrument?

Key Instrument Attributes:

  • Usability – This is how easy the instrument is to administer, complete, and score.
  • Alignment – This is how well the instrument’s identified purpose reflects your ideal outcome.
  • Validity – This is whether the instrument measures what it is supposed to measure.
  • Reliability – This is how consistently the instrument measures what you are interested in.
